Rimworld Movement Speed Guide: Slow Down Your Raiders

Do you want to enhance your defense strategy in Rimworld? Slowing down raiders can be a game-changer when it comes to defending your colony. In this guide, we will explore various techniques to reduce the movement speed of raiders, ensuring that your defenses hold strong.

Understanding the Movement Speed System#

Before we delve into the different techniques, let’s first understand how movement speed works in Rimworld. In version 1.2, a mod called “mod mode” was introduced, providing a movement speed reduction of 25. However, there seems to be a minimum threshold in place, preventing colonists from moving any slower. Essentially, this means that the mod mode can only slow down raiders to a certain extent.

The Impact of Terrain#

In the past, using different types of chunks and items to create obstacles was an effective strategy for slowing down raiders. However, in the latest version, this technique has been nerfed. Aligning different types of chunks or items no longer makes any difference in movement speed. However, the terrain still plays a significant role in slowing down raiders.

Among the vanilla terrains, marshes are the slowest. Moving through marshes will considerably reduce the movement speed of colonists. Additionally, terrains such as snow or foam can further slow down colonists, but they can be challenging to obtain and somewhat redundant since the movement speed is already capped at 25.

Lighting and Movement Speed#

Did you know that lighting conditions can also affect movement speed in Rimworld? As shown in the mod mode example, lighting has an impact on movement speed. Darker paths can reduce the colonist’s speed, bringing them closer to the speed cap. By strategically creating darker paths using chunks and items, you can slow down raiders even further.

Slow Terrain Types and their Viability#

While marshes are the slowest terrain, they can be difficult to acquire and manipulate. A more practical alternative is thick snow. Thick snow is easier to obtain than marshes and offers a considerable reduction in movement speed. However, it’s important to keep the area cold, as warm temperatures will melt the snow and nullify the slow speed effect. Despite this, it’s worth noting that the difference between thick snow and standard soil is minimal.

The Role of Furniture#

Lastly, let’s discuss the impact of furniture on movement speed. Surprisingly, furniture such as pots, animals, and sleeping boxes do not affect movement speed. However, it’s interesting to note that stools and tables can actually speed up colonists, having the opposite effect. Therefore, it’s advisable to avoid using these furniture pieces when attempting to slow down raiders.

Conclusion#

In conclusion, while the old go-to strategies of using different types of chunks and items have been nerfed in the latest version of Rimworld, there are still effective ways to slow down raiders and enhance your defense strategy. Marshes, if available, offer the slowest movement speed, but thick snow can be a viable alternative. Creating darker paths by considering lighting conditions can also further reduce the movement speed of raiders. Remember to avoid using furniture pieces that can speed up colonists, such as stools and tables.

By implementing these techniques, you can create formidable defenses that will give your colony the upper hand when facing raids. So why wait? Start incorporating these strategies into your gameplay and see the difference it makes!
