How to Easily Root Moto G4 & G4 Plus

Rooting your Moto G4 & G4 Plus can give you more control over your device and allow you to customize your Android experience. In this article, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of rooting your device using minimal ADB and fastboot. By following these instructions, you can unlock the full potential of your Moto G4 & G4 Plus. So, without wasting any time, let’s get started.

Download the Required Files#

Before you begin, make sure to download the necessary files. These files can be found in the description below the video. Once you have downloaded the files, you will have a zip file containing four files. Extract the files and you will find the “Minimal ADB and Fastboot” setup. Run this setup as an administrator and go through the installation process by clicking “Next” and accepting the terms. Once the installation is complete, you will find the “Minimal ADB and Fastboot” folder in your program files.

Prepare Your Device#

To get started with the rooting process, you need to reboot your device into fastboot mode. Power off your device and then press the volume down button and power button simultaneously to enter fastboot mode. Once your device is in fastboot mode, connect it to your PC using a USB cable.

Connect Your Device and Open Minimal ADB and Fastboot#

After connecting your device to your PC, open the “Minimal ADB and Fastboot” folder and double-click on the CMD file. This will open a command prompt window. In the command prompt window, type the command “fastboot devices” to check if your device is successfully connected. You should see your device’s name displayed in the window.

Flash the Recovery#

To flash the recovery, type the following command in the command prompt window: “fastboot flash recovery [recovery file name].img”. Make sure to rename the recovery file to match the file name you copied earlier. After typing the command, press enter and the recovery will be flashed on your device.

Reboot to Recovery Mode#

Once the recovery is flashed, you need to reboot your device to recovery mode. Use the volume up and down buttons to navigate to the “recovery mode” option and press the power button to select it. Wait for your device to reboot into recovery mode.

Copy Files and Install Elemental X Kernel#

Now, reconnect your device to your PC using the USB cable. In the “Minimal ADB and Fastboot” folder, copy the “Superuser” and “Elemental External” files. After copying the files, go back to the recovery mode. In the recovery mode, navigate to the “install” section and select the two files you just copied. First, flash the “Elemental X Kernel” file by clicking on it and swiping to confirm. Once the flashing process is complete, click “Next” and agree to the terms of use.

Final Steps#

Next, select “USB Pass-Through Mode” and click “Next”. Now, click “Install Elemental Eyelets” and wait for the installation to finish. Once it’s done, click “Next” and then “Finish”. Now, you need to flash the “Super User” file. Select “Super User” and flash it. After the file is successfully flashed, go to the “reboot” section and select “system”. Let your device reboot to the system.

Verify Root Access#

After your device has finished rebooting, check if it is properly rooted. You will not find any root app in your app drawer. To verify root access, go to the Play Store and search for “PHH SuperUser Root Management”. Install the app and open it. You should now see the app in your app drawer, indicating that your device is properly rooted.

Congratulations! You have successfully rooted your Moto G4 & G4 Plus. Enjoy the freedom and customization options that come with rooting your device. If you encounter any issues during the process, feel free to leave a comment, and we will be happy to assist you.
