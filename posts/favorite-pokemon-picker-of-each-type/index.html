Ultimate Favorite Pokemon Picker for Each Generation, Type & More!

Welcome to the ultimate guide for picking your favorite Pokemon from each generation, based on type and other factors! In this article, we will go through each generation and break down the best Pokemon for each type, as well as discuss some of the fan-favorite legendaries and gimmick forms. Whether you’re a Gen 1 purist or a fan of the newer generations, we have got you covered. Let’s dive in!

Generation 1: The Originals#

Let’s start with the first generation of Pokemon. When it comes to choosing a favorite starter, the options are tough. But for our expert copywriter, Bulbasaur takes the crown. In terms of starters, Totodile, Treecko, and Snivy all have their merits, but Snivy wins this round. Moving on to other types, Dragonite takes the title of the favorite dragon, while Cloyster shines as the best ice type. Machamp steals the show as the favorite fighting type.

In terms of rock types, Aerodactyl exhibits its coolness perfectly. Grass types bring about another tough decision, but Venusaur wins out. For poison types, wheezing reigns supreme. Scyther is the top choice for bug types, and Clefable takes the crown for fairy types. And let’s not forget about Zapdos, the favorite flying type. Articuno earns a special mention as the best legendary.

Generation 2: The Johto Region#

Moving on to the second generation, it’s time to pick the favorites all over again. For the normal type, Ursaring stands out. Chandelure lights up the competition as the best fire type. Water types bring about a controversial choice, but Kingdra comes out on top. What about bug types? Scizor takes the cake.

Psychic types present a tough decision, but Celebi ultimately wins. Dark types, on the other hand, have a clear frontrunner in Tyranitar. When it comes to rock types, Steelix steals the spotlight. And for ground types, it’s a tough call, but Steelix wins again.

Generation 3: The Hoenn Region#

Now let’s move on to the third generation and dive into the favorites once more. Slaking is the clear winner for normal types, while Blaziken takes the crown for fire types. Water types open up a debate between Clamperl and Huntail, but Surskit ultimately comes out on top. In the bug category, Ninjask takes the lead.

Favorite psychic types once again bring about a tough decision, but Baltoy squeaks by as the winner. Dark types see Sharpedo as the uncontested favorite. For rock types, it’s a tie between Armaldo and Regirock. And speaking of legendaries, Latias wins the title of favorite dragon.

Generation 4: The Sinnoh Region#

In the fourth generation, Arceus takes the cake as the top normal type. Chandelure shines yet again as the favorite fire type. Electric types bring about a debate between Rotom and Helioptile, but Rotom takes the win. The grass type favorite goes to Roserade, while Azelf steals the show as the best psychic type. Mothim surprisingly comes out on top as the favorite bug type.

When it comes to legendaries, Arceus secures its spot as the favorite. And for the overall favorite Pokemon of this generation, it has to be Chandelure.

Generation 5: The Unova Region#

Favorite normal types in the fifth generation lead us to Meloetta. Fire types bring up the ever-cool Chandelure once again. Water types cause a dilemma between Basculin and Samurott, but Samurott triumphs. The ghost type favorite goes to Chandelure yet again, while Haxorus tops the charts as the best dragon type. Durant steals the spotlight as the best steel type.

Generation 6: The Kalos Region#

Finally, we reach the last generation covered in this article. The normal type favorite is Mega Kangaskhan, while Charizard wins the title for fire types. Electric types bring about a tough decision, but Helioptile ultimately triumphs. Grass types see Venusaur as the frontrunner. The grand favorite of this generation goes to Gothitelle.

And with that, we have reached the end of our ultimate favorite Pokemon picker. We hope this guide helped you discover or rediscover your love for these incredible creatures. Remember, picking favorites is never an easy task, but it’s always fun to explore the different possibilities and spark conversations among trainers. Happy hunting!
